# BlackRock hackathons in India: winning projects, tech stacks, and insider insights

**BlackRock runs three distinct hackathon programs touching India — an internal global event (HACK:BLK), a campus series for students (HackKnight), and a professional recruitment challenge (Hacking India) — collectively drawing thousands of participants and producing solutions that have become real products like Aladdin Wealth.** India punches well above its weight in these events: roughly **40% of all hackathon ideas** in recent HACK:BLK editions originated from India-based teams, and the India-Japan team won the 2023 global hackathon. The themes consistently orbit financial technology, Aladdin platform innovation, AI/ML applications, and financial literacy — reflecting BlackRock's core business priorities. Below is everything publicly available about winning projects, tech choices, and what separates winners from the rest.

## Three hackathon programs serve different audiences

BlackRock's hackathon ecosystem in India operates across three tiers. **HACK:BLK**, the flagship internal employee hackathon, launched around 2015 and celebrated its 10th anniversary in 2024. It spans all regions and business functions, with India-based employees in Gurugram, Mumbai, and Bengaluru forming cross-regional teams with colleagues worldwide. Teams virtually plan a project, then execute a two-day sprint to build a working prototype. Finalists create video presentations and the entire firm votes to select winners.

**HackKnight** is BlackRock India's student-facing 24-hour hackathon, held simultaneously at Indian campuses. The 2024 edition ran at VIT Pune and Thapar Institute of Engineering and Technology, drawing **440 participants across 110 teams** who produced 108 working prototypes. Seven teams won — four at VIT Pune, three at Thapar. The 2023 edition also ran at Thapar University. These events serve as a recruitment pipeline, with several HackKnight participants later joining BlackRock as analysts.

The newest addition, **Hacking India 2026**, targets experienced software engineers with 3–7 years of experience. Hosted on HackerRank, it launched on February 20, 2026, with a 7-hour coding challenge on February 21. Participants must submit a public Git repository with a Dockerfile, a Docker container image running on **port 5477**, and a 3–5 minute video demo. The theme: "Building Sustainable Financial Well-Being."

## Winning projects that became real products

The most consequential winners have crossed the gap from prototype to production. **Aladdin Wealth**, now a fully operational solution used by wealth managers globally, originated as a HACK:BLK hackathon concept. **BLK4U (BlackRock for Universities)**, born from Sam Konigsberg's idea at the 2020 Atlanta hackathon, now operates at **45+ schools** across the U.S. and Europe, teaching portfolio construction using Aladdin's Portfolio 360 tool.

The **2023 HACK:BLK winner, Smart Sensei**, was built by a cross-regional team led by Mael Pidjou (Associate, Aladdin Client Business, Tokyo) that included **three colleagues from India**. Smart Sensei applied generative AI and machine learning to search BlackRock's Aladdin data reservoirs more intuitively — essentially building a ChatGPT-style interface for internal financial data retrieval. The project caught the attention of an AI team in London for potential production implementation. The team deliberately assembled diverse skills: ML/language model engineers, an analyst with machine learning interest, and a Director providing project management.

The **2020 HACK:BLK produced two winners** for the first time in the event's history. **Themantic** (the firm-wide vote winner) used state-of-the-art NLP to process corporate disclosures and automatically create thematic portfolios aligned with investor values — sustainability, growth, robotics, education. **Aladdin Eye** (the Global Operating Committee winner) built a flexible search and visualization platform for financial analysts, specifically targeting Private Markets documents for extracting investment and due diligence insights. That year saw **1,200 registered participants submit 500 ideas**, with 192 coming from cross-regional teams.

## Student projects from HackKnight reveal common patterns

Among publicly documented HackKnight projects, **FinEdge** stands out from the 2023 edition. Built by a Thapar University team (Arunain Mahant, Jahanavi Khattar, and Govind), it was a comprehensive fintech application featuring an AI-powered chatbot, a Knowledge Hub with courses and blogs for financial literacy, a trading dashboard with real-time stock analysis, and **FinGames** — gamified learning experiences. The multi-feature approach reflected the judges' preference for holistic solutions.

**Wealthify**, built by Kedarnath Rothe during a 24-hour BlackRock hackathon in July 2024, is one of the few projects with a publicly accessible GitHub repository and live demo. Deployed at wealthify01.vercel.app, it provides financial education and management for students through speech-to-text course queries, a top-5 course recommendation engine, WhatsApp integration for personalized suggestions, and a "Call Me" feature for phone-based course explanations. Its multi-channel approach — web, WhatsApp, and phone — demonstrated the kind of user-centric thinking that resonates with judges.

Notable HackKnight participants who have surfaced on LinkedIn include Samruddhi Nande (now Analyst at BlackRock, from Vishwakarma Institute of Information Technology, Pune, who placed in the top 9), Devansh Garg (listed as "BlackRock Hackathon Runner Up" from Thapar Institute, now Software Engineer at Blue Yonder), and Nalin Singhal (listed as a HackKnight 2024 Finalist from Thapar).

## Tech stacks lean toward Python, Java, and AI/ML frameworks

Across all BlackRock hackathon programs, a clear technology pattern emerges. The officially supported languages for Hacking India 2026 are **Java, Python, Go, Rust, C, C++, and .NET**, with Docker containerization now a hard requirement. The most common stacks seen in winning and notable projects break down as follows:

- **AI/ML dominates recent winners**: Smart Sensei used generative AI and large language models; Themantic leveraged NLP on corporate disclosures; FinEdge integrated an AI chatbot; Wealthify used Python's speech recognition library for voice-first UX
- **Python and Java are the backbone**: Wealthify's backend runs Python; the RoboAdvisor (BlackRock Hackathon Award winner at HackGT 2019) used Node.js with Python scripts; BlackRock CodeSprint solutions on GitHub are in Java
- **Frontend and deployment**: Vercel for quick deployment (Wealthify), Microsoft Azure (RoboAdvisor), Docker containers (Hacking India 2026 requirement)
- **BlackRock Aladdin REST API** is the critical differentiator — teams that creatively leveraged Aladdin's proprietary data for risk management, portfolio construction, or intelligent search consistently outperformed

The **RoboAdvisor project** (by team SzechuanTech, 2017), which won the BlackRock Hackathon Award at an external event, offers a candid technical lesson. Built with Node.js on Microsoft Bot Framework and deployed on Azure, it used the Aladdin API to create a risk-profiling chatbot for individual investors. The team noted that Aladdin API documentation was sparse, requiring significant reverse-engineering: "Documentation of a framework or API is nearly as crucial as how well the technology is actually implemented."

## What separates winners from the rest

Synthesizing insights from judges, organizers, and winners across multiple years, several patterns define winning solutions.

**Cross-functional, cross-regional teams consistently outperform.** The 2020 HACK:BLK data is telling: 85% of teams included members from outside their direct team, 50% had members from multiple offices, and 30% spanned geographic regions. Smart Sensei's India-Japan collaboration was central to its success. Mael Pidjou reflected: "The hackathon really proved to me that BlackRock has so many skillful people all over and that it's possible to collaborate with different teams and different regions."

**Production potential matters more than flashiness.** Mimi Narbonne, VP on the Aladdin Engineering COO team and a key organizer, noted that "a lot of times, it's not a flashy project you see in the headlines" — some ideas that didn't win first place have become real businesses within Aladdin. Judges evaluate whether a prototype could realistically scale into a production tool. The 2026 Hacking India event makes this explicit by requiring Docker containerization and production-ready code.

**Domain-specific innovation trumps generic technical prowess.** Winning solutions demonstrate deep understanding of BlackRock's business — Themantic understood how corporate disclosures map to thematic investing, Smart Sensei identified a real pain point in Aladdin data retrieval, and Aladdin Eye targeted the specific workflow of Private Markets analysts. For student hackathons, financial literacy and education themes dominate, and projects that combine financial domain knowledge with creative technology application stand out.

**Judging involves both executive review and democratic voting.** HACK:BLK uses a dual-track system: a Global Operating Committee of senior leaders selects one winner, while the entire firm votes for another. This means winning projects must appeal both to technical experts and to business-side employees who evaluate practical impact.

## Conclusion

BlackRock's hackathon ecosystem in India has matured from a single internal innovation event into a three-tier pipeline spanning students, employees, and experienced professionals. The through-line across all programs is **Aladdin platform fluency** — teams that demonstrate creative command of BlackRock's proprietary technology stack consistently rise to the top. The shift toward generative AI since 2023 (Smart Sensei's win marked a turning point) and the increasing emphasis on production-readiness (Docker requirements, containerized deployments) signal where future winning solutions will need to compete. For aspiring participants, the clearest strategic advantage is combining deep financial domain knowledge with emerging AI capabilities, assembling diverse cross-functional teams, and building prototypes that solve real operational pain points rather than impressive-but-impractical demos. India's outsized contribution — nearly half of all ideas in recent HACK:BLK editions — suggests the country's teams have already internalized these lessons.

# BlackRock hackathon winners: every project, tech stack, and winning pattern decoded

**Aladdin Wealth — now a $1B+ annual revenue business — started as a HACK:BLK hackathon project around 2018, making it the single most successful hackathon-to-product conversion in BlackRock's history.** Across BlackRock's internal HACK:BLK events, external HackKnight series, and sponsored prizes at collegiate hackathons, a clear pattern emerges: winning projects solve universal pain points with working prototypes that leverage AI/ML on top of Aladdin's data infrastructure. This research covers **6 confirmed HACK:BLK winners, 4 confirmed external prize winners, and 5+ additional notable projects** with full technical details, code repos, and architecture decisions.

BlackRock runs two distinct hackathon tracks: the internal **HACK:BLK** (started ~2015, 10th anniversary in 2024) for all employees globally, and external events including **HackKnight** (India campus recruitment), **Hacking India** (HackerRank), sponsored API prizes at collegiate hackathons (HackGT, CalHacks, UGAHacks, HackKings), and regional events (Budapest, Milan). The internal HACK:BLK winners are selected through a combination of Global Operating Committee (GOC) votes by senior leaders and firm-wide employee voting, while external events typically judge based on working prototypes and innovative use of BlackRock's Aladdin API.

---

## The crown jewel: Aladdin Wealth went from hackathon to billion-dollar product

**Aladdin Wealth** originated from a HACK:BLK hackathon around 2017–2018 and represents BlackRock's most dramatic hackathon success story. A BlackRock job posting confirmed: "Aladdin Wealth was born three years ago as a BlackRock hackathon project and has rapidly expanded as a product used by some of the largest wealth managers in the world." The platform enables wealth managers to accelerate growth, drive advisor efficiency through connected digital systems, and personalize client portfolios with risk oversight, model management, scaled portfolio rebalancing, and next-best-action recommendations. Clients now include **Citi, Morgan Stanley, and Allianz Bank**. The Aladdin WealthTech unit generates approximately **$1 billion in annual revenue**. No public code exists — it's a fully commercial enterprise product — but a job listing noted: "You will be working with some of the original participants in the hackathon."

**BLK4U (BlackRock for Universities)** is the second major hackathon-to-real-world success. **Sam Konigsberg**, Director in U.S. Wealth Advisory (Atlanta), pitched the concept at the 2020 HACK:BLK. He had noticed that university Student-Managed Investment Funds (SMIFs) lacked access to professional tools — some used Bloomberg Terminal, others just Excel. BLK4U became a four-week course teaching students to use Aladdin's 360 Evaluator Tool for portfolio construction, with each weekly session taught by a different BlackRock employee. With **Marc Balizer** (Managing Director, Aladdin Atlanta) as sponsor and **Emiere Scaife** expanding it to HBCUs (Morehouse, Spelman, Winston-Salem State, Hampton), the program now operates at **45 schools across the U.S. and Europe**. This is notable because it won not as a technology product but as an educational program — proof that HACK:BLK judges value real-world impact over technical sophistication alone.

## HACK:BLK internal winners from 2020–2023 share an AI-first DNA

### Themantic (2020 — firm-wide vote winner)

Themantic harnessed **state-of-the-art NLP to process corporate disclosures** (10-Ks, annual reports) and automatically create thematic portfolios aligned with investor values — sustainability, growth, robotics, education. The innovation was making thematic portfolio construction systematic and scalable rather than manual. It won the firm-wide employee vote during the first fully remote HACK:BLK (COVID-19), which attracted **1,200 participants submitting 500 ideas**. The 2020 hackathon introduced dual judging tracks for the first time: the GOC senior leadership vote and the all-employee vote. Themantic aligned perfectly with BlackRock's "tech2025" goals around using data for better portfolios, and the growing market demand for ESG/thematic investing. No public code exists (internal project), but the architecture was an AI-driven document processing pipeline mapping companies to thematic criteria at scale.

### Aladdin Eye (2020 — GOC favorite)

The second 2020 winner, chosen by BlackRock's Global Operating Committee of senior leaders, was **Aladdin Eye** — a flexible, scalable platform for performing search tasks and visualizing results intuitively for financial analysts. Its killer application was extracting insights from **Private Markets documents** that were never before available for investing and due diligence. The architecture combined search technology, NLP-based document intelligence, and data visualization tailored for analyst workflows. While not publicly confirmed as productized, BlackRock subsequently expanded Private Markets capabilities significantly (acquiring eFront, launching pre-investment analytics), suggesting Aladdin Eye's direction influenced the roadmap.

### Bee BlackRock (2021 — Belgrade team)

The 2021 winner was **Bee BlackRock**, built by a six-person team from BlackRock's Belgrade, Serbia office: **Tanja Babic, Dimitrije Veljkovic, Filip Cordas, Gordana Pejic, Kristina Stanojevic, and Nenad Nikolic**. It was a **mobile platform using gamification** to encourage employees to achieve sustainable goals — decreasing carbon footprints and promoting diversity, equity, and inclusion. Gordana explained: "We thought we could make something all of us could use." The project became real: BlackRock partnered with its SustainaBLK network to implement a **Net Zero challenge app** for all employees. A video titled "Advancing Sustainability | 2021 Hackathon" was published on BlackRock's YouTube (ID: Js9qq9KtqsA). The sustainability theme dominated 2021, and Bee BlackRock won because it combined a universal use case with engaging gamification mechanics.

### Smart Sensei (2023 — overall winner)

The most recent confirmed winner, **Smart Sensei**, used **generative AI and large language models** to search Aladdin's database more efficiently. Led by **Mael Pidjou**, an Associate on the Aladdin Client Business team in Tokyo (only 6 months into his role when he pitched the idea), the team of six spanned Japan and India:

- Three members from Tokyo (inspiring the "Sensei" name)
- Three colleagues from India
- An Analyst who studied ML in college
- Two engineers expert in machine learning and language models
- A Director providing project management experience

The 2023 theme was **"Operational Excellence"** — the broadest ever, generating the most submissions in HACK:BLK history. Smart Sensei won because it addressed a pain point everyone recognized: "Almost every person I had discussed my problem with said it was a pain point for them, too." Post-hackathon, the use case "caught the eyes of an AI team in London," and Mael was collaborating toward beta testing. The tech stack centered on generative AI/LLMs applied to Aladdin's internal data infrastructure — essentially a ChatGPT-style interface for investment management data. No public repo exists, but the architecture was a natural-language search interface layered over Aladdin's data reservoirs.

## External hackathon winners reveal what BlackRock judges reward at collegiate events

Four confirmed external prize winners have public GitHub repositories, offering direct insight into winning technical approaches:

**STRAT / RoboAdvisor (HackGT ~2017, "BlackRock Hackathon Award")** — Built by team SzechuanTech led by **James Gan** (Cornell senior). A chatbot bringing professional risk management to individual investors via personality-based risk profiling. Tech stack: **Node.js (Microsoft Bot Framework), Python backend scripts, BlackRock Aladdin REST API, Microsoft Azure hosting**, integrated with Slack/Messenger/Skype. The bot asks personality questions to calculate a risk score (0–1), then Python scripts fetch security data and risk metrics from the Aladdin API to generate personalized recommendations. The team noted API documentation was sparse: "Definitions of the terms on the Aladdin API were few and far between, so we had to do a fair bit of research." Repo: github.com/jamesylgan/RoboAdvisor (21 stars, MIT license, 106 commits). Devpost: devpost.com/software/szechuantech.

**Portfolio Optimizer (HackGT 2019, "1st Place, Best Use of BlackRock's API")** — Built by the antm team (GitHub: Piejak/antm-hackgt-2019). A web app providing **Sharpe ratio-optimized portfolio allocation** based on user risk tolerance and investment time horizon. Tech stack: **React, JavaScript**, with BlackRock's Aladdin API for financial data. The core innovation was automating professional-grade portfolio optimization math (Sharpe ratio maximization) in an accessible web interface. Repo: github.com/Piejak/antm-hackgt-2019 (3 stars).

**br-pdv (CalHacks 6.0, "BlackRock API Prize")** — Built by **Richard Zhu** (github.com/richardszhu/br-pdv). A portfolio diversity visualization tool using **Python and MatPlotLib**. It queried BlackRock's Aladdin API to analyze portfolio diversity across security attributes (Industry, Sector, Country, Asset Type) and generated visual charts. Key files: `portfolio.py`, `vis.py`, `vis_notebook.ipynb`. Notable caveat: "Unfortunately, it can no longer be demoed as BlackRock has since closed off the API from the public." Repo has 4 stars and 89 commits.

**Mo-Choo (HackKings 2022, "Winner, BlackRock Technical Challenge")** — Built by **Kenza Amira** and team at King's College London. ## What actually wins: the pattern across all BlackRock hackathons

Synthesizing every confirmed winner reveals **six consistent differentiators**:

- **Universal pain points beat niche innovations.** Smart Sensei won because "almost every person said it was a pain point." Aladdin Wealth succeeded because every wealth manager needed the tool. Build something everyone recognizes as solving their daily frustration.
- **Working prototypes are mandatory.** Organizer Mimi Narbonne stated explicitly: "In the end, you need a working prototype." Every winner delivered functional demos, not slide decks. For HACK:BLK, finalists create video presentations of working software.
- **Cross-regional, cross-functional teams win disproportionately.** In 2020, 85% of teams included members from outside their direct team, 50% spanned offices, 30% spanned regions. Smart Sensei's Japan-India team, Bee BlackRock's Belgrade squad, and BLK4U's Atlanta team all demonstrated "One BlackRock" collaboration.
- **Alignment with firm strategy is table stakes.** Themantic aligned with ESG/thematic investing demand. Smart Sensei aligned with AI democratization. Bee BlackRock aligned with net-zero goals. Every winner mapped to the year's strategic theme (sustainability, operational excellence, tech2025).
- **Productization potential matters enormously.** Judges and the GOC evaluate whether a project could become a real product. Aladdin Wealth, BLK4U, and Bee BlackRock all became real. Mimi Narbonne noted: "The team didn't come in first place, but their idea is now a real business."
- **AI/ML is the dominant winning technology.** Themantic (NLP), Smart Sensei (GenAI/LLM), Aladdin Eye (document intelligence), and most external winners used AI. For the current Hacking India 2026, this means AI-driven solutions for sustainable financial well-being will have a structural advantage.

## Conclusion

The strongest signal from this research is that **BlackRock hackathon winners are not technically exotic — they are technically sound solutions to universally recognized problems.** Aladdin Wealth didn't win with novel algorithms; it won by making institutional-grade portfolio management accessible to wealth managers. Smart Sensei didn't invent new AI; it applied existing GenAI to a search problem everyone hated. The winning formula is: identify a widespread pain point → build a working prototype that leverages AI/ML on Aladdin's data → form a cross-functional team → align the narrative to BlackRock's current strategic priorities → demonstrate clear productization potential. For the current Hacking India 2026 ("Building Sustainable Financial Well-Being"), this means the winning project will likely combine AI-driven personalization with retirement/savings optimization, delivered in a Dockerized container on port 5477, with a compelling 3–5 minute demo video showing real user impact.
